# Virtual Experiment 1 – Geometrical and Wave Optics 

We have a prism like the opposite figure, whose base is an equilateral triangle. If a light ray is incident on it with an angle of incidence $\alpha$, it deviates from its initial path by an amount $D$. When the path inside the prism is symmetrical, $D$ is minimized. We denote its minimum by $D_m$, and it can be proven that the following relationship holds:

$$2\sin\left(\frac{D_m + \pi/3}{2}\right) = n$$



---

Young's Double Slit: 

There are also **7** lasers at your disposal, each with a different wavelength. It can be proven that if you shine the laser onto an opaque sheet that has two very close narrow slits, an interference pattern is created on a screen at a distance $L$ from it. It can be proven that the distance between adjacent bright or dark fringes is equal to $\frac{\lambda L}{d}$.

* In the experiment, the distance from the screen to the sheet is measured as **2.17 ± 0.01m**.


* The slit spacing is $d$ = **10µm** without error.



---

Section (A) 

Find the wavelength of each of the lasers numbered **1** to **7** using Young's double-slit experiment. The program gives you the distance between two adjacent fringes. Note that there is random error in the measurements of this section, and measuring a quantity multiple times will result in several different measured values. Assume that our measuring instrument has an error of **0.01**.
To exit section A, input `exit` to the program.

---

Section (B) 

For each of the lasers, find the prism's refractive index $n$ by collecting appropriate data (8 or more = full grade) and plotting their graphs. The refractive index is different for each laser. When entering $\alpha$, you cannot go outside the range of **-90** to **+90**, and you must enter a number that is a multiple of **0.1**. Entering more decimal places has no effect. Whenever the program outputs "Total internal reflection was observed", it means total reflection has occurred. In this section, the program's output has no random error. Take the measurement error of $D$ to be **0.001**. There is no random error either.

---

Section (C) 

According to Cauchy's equation $n(\lambda) = A + \frac{B}{\lambda^2}$:
Determine the validity of the relationship by plotting a graph and performing a regression. If the relationship holds, find $A$ and $B$.
