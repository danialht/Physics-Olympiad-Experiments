# Virtual Experiment Number 2

Assume you are on another planet whose characteristics are not necessarily identical to those of Earth. You have a ball that you can hang from a point; assume your string is very thin so we neglect its dimensions, and a very small hole has been created on the ball that allows the string to enter it (as shown in the figure) and attach to the center of the ball. 

You can use balls with different densities. The program asks you for the density and radius of the ball, which you can input. The precision of the density you enter is 100 kg/m³, and entering a more precise density has no effect, as the program will ignore it (for example, 1499 is treated as 1500); air friction is a factor simulated in the program. You can change the length of the string from which it hangs. The air friction force is proportional to the cross-sectional area and the speed relative to the air. The goal of the experiment is to find the friction proportionality constant c and the gravitational acceleration of this planet g. 

### Experiment Steps

**a)** Find g with appropriate data collection, and also draw a graph. 

**b)** Assuming no friction, find the period T in terms of the amplitude $\theta_0$ up to the first non-zero order. 

**c)** We want to find g once again using the answer from the previous section, but with a different method. Release the pendulum from an angle and simultaneously start your stopwatch; when the pendulum returns and reaches a momentary stop, record the time, wait for it to go and return again, and record the time again at the momentary stop... and then repeat this experiment again, but this time record the amplitude angles. Choose the number of periods during which you collect data yourself. Repeat this process three times (a table has been provided for you for this section). Find g and draw a graph. 

**d)** Assume the effect of friction is small; find the oscillation amplitude $\theta_0$ in terms of time up to the first order of the friction coefficient. Perform the calculations up to the first non-zero order of $\theta_0$. 

**e)** Perform the previous section up to the second non-zero order of $\theta_0$. 

**f)** Find c with new data collection or with the help of your previous data, according to part (e). Also, draw a graph. 

**g)** Turn on the fan. When you turn on the fan, a wind is generated at all points in front of the fan, the speed of which is related to the distance from the fan. Find the dependence of wind speed on distance, draw a graph, and fill out a table.

### Program Guide

The program screen is as follows. 

![Program screen showing the protractor, ruler, stopwatch, and control buttons](/experiments/exp2/Picture1.png)

**HOLD Button**: The HOLD button acts like the HOLD on a real stopwatch. It freezes the time on the stopwatch display, but time continues to pass in its memory; by pressing it again, you will see the current time. 


**RESET Button**: The RESET button resets the stopwatch to 0:0:0 regardless of its current state. 
 
**START Button**: The START button is like the START/STOP button on a real stopwatch. Pressing it stops the stopwatch. 
 
**FAN ON/OFF Button**: The FAN ON/OFF button brings a fan onto the screen that you can move with your mouse. 

**Final Notes:** The precision of the angle you provide to the program is 1 degree, and the length of the string and the radius are 1 centimeter. Good luck.
